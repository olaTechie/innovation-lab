Every year, 4.6 billion people wake up without access to essential health services. Not because the science does not exist. Because the systems to deliver it have failed them.

[PAUSE]

Welcome to Mission Control.

[VISUAL CUE: Animated map of the fictional nation of Asante fading in, four distinct regions glowing]

You are about to enter a simulation. Not a textbook exercise. A pressure test. You will be dropped into the Republic of Asante -- a landlocked nation of 12.4 million people in sub-Saharan Africa. Life expectancy: 58 years. Health spending: 47 dollars per person per year. Less than a physician for every ten thousand people. Forty-five percent of rural health facilities run out of essential drugs every quarter.

Asante has four regions, and three of them are in crisis.

[VISUAL CUE: Map zooms into each region as it is named]

The Kibo Highlands. Two million people scattered across mountain villages. Average travel time to a hospital: three hours. Mothers are dying in childbirth at five times the national rate.

Lumasa Metro. The capital. 3.8 million people. Glass towers above sprawling informal settlements. Tuberculosis spreading silently through overcrowded housing. A dengue outbreak doubling by the month. Hospitals at 142 percent capacity.

The Lake Amara Basin. 2.3 million people, including 180,000 refugees. Climate displacement. Chronic disease. Community tensions. International funding dropping 12 percent every year.

[PAUSE]

Here is the thing. You will not face these crises as yourself. You will be assigned a role.

[VISUAL CUE: Six role cards appearing one by one]

Minister of Health -- you hold the budget and answer to Parliament. NGO Director -- your donors demand community impact, but your funding has an expiration date. Community Health Worker -- you know which programs actually work on the ground, and which ones communities will reject. Tech Entrepreneur -- you believe innovation can leapfrog infrastructure, but connectivity in the highlands is 12 percent. WHO Regional Advisor -- you push for evidence-based solutions and worry about equity gaps that keep widening. Patient Advocate -- you fight for dignity, access, and the voices that never reach the planning table.

Each role sees different information. Each role has different priorities. The same decision will feel right to one stakeholder and catastrophic to another.

[PAUSE]

You will face three scenarios. Three escalating crises. In each one, you will make decisions that ripple across six metrics: coverage, equity, cost-effectiveness, sustainability, community trust, and health outcomes. There are no perfect answers. Every choice involves a trade-off.

[VISUAL CUE: Six metric gauges animating from zero]

Scenario One -- The Silent Epidemic. Maternal mortality in the mountains. Scenario Two -- The Invisible Threat. TB and dengue colliding in a megacity. Scenario Three -- The Long Game. Climate change, refugees, and a health system that must outlast its funding.

[PAUSE]

This simulation is built on real data. Real innovations. Real failures. The numbers come from WHO, the World Bank, the Lancet, and field programs operating right now across sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia. The choices you face are the choices health leaders face every day.

So. Your briefing packet is loaded. Your role is assigned. Your country needs a strategy.

[VISUAL CUE: Mission Control dashboard powering up]

Welcome to Asante. The clock is running.

[PAUSE]

Select your role. Begin your mission.
