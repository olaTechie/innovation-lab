Two epidemics. One city. Not enough beds for either.

[PAUSE]

[VISUAL CUE: Aerial view of Lumasa Metro -- glass towers above dense informal settlement rooftops, hospital buildings with overcrowding indicators]

Lumasa Metro is under dual siege. Asante's capital, 3.8 million people. Forty percent of them packed into informal settlements with intermittent water, poor ventilation, and overcrowded housing. The city's two referral hospitals were built for a million. They now serve nearly four million. Bed occupancy: 142 percent.

Tuberculosis has been spreading quietly through these settlements for years. Last year, 809 cases were notified. Epidemiological models say the real number is over 1,100. One in four cases is going undetected. Worse -- drug-resistant TB has been found in 8 percent of new cases. Triple the national average. Every patient who starts treatment and stops early creates the conditions for resistance to spread.

[VISUAL CUE: Stat cards -- 73% TB detection rate, 8% MDR-TB, 2,340 dengue cases this month, 142% hospital capacity]

And then there is the dengue outbreak. Climate change has extended the mosquito breeding season by six weeks. Construction sites have created standing water everywhere. Cases doubled in the past month alone -- 2,340 this month and climbing. The media is running daily death counts. The public is panicking.

[PAUSE]

Here is the tension. TB will kill ten times more people over the next five years. But dengue is killing people right now, visibly, on the evening news. The mayor has declared a dual health emergency. You have three million dollars. Every dollar you spend on TB is a dollar you do not spend on dengue. And vice versa.

[VISUAL CUE: Split screen -- TB spreading invisibly in dark settlement interiors vs. dengue patient in overcrowded emergency ward]

In Indonesia, portable X-ray units and the 99DOTS adherence system transformed TB detection -- two dollars per patient. In Yogyakarta, Wolbachia mosquitoes cut dengue cases by 77 percent and the effect lasted eight years without re-intervention. The tools exist. But which threat do you prioritize when you cannot afford to fight both at full strength?

[PAUSE]

The clock is running. The settlements cannot wait. Make your call.
